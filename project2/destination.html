PLACES TO GO

NEW DELHI

Jantar Mantar in Delhi is an ancient astronomical observatory built in the 18th century by Maharaja Jai Singh II. It houses 13 impressive instruments designed to observe celestial movements and measure time accurately. The highlight is the Giant Sundial, which casts a shadow to indicate the time of day. Other instruments like the Jai Prakash Yantra and Ram Yantra showcase India's advanced knowledge in astronomy. Today, Jantar Mantar stands as a symbol of India's rich scientific heritage, attracting visitors from all over the world.

UTTAR PRADESH

The Taj Mahal in Uttar Pradesh is a renowned monument built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Constructed from white marble over 22 years, it's celebrated for its stunning architecture, intricate craftsmanship, and romantic significance. This UNESCO World Heritage Site attracts millions of visitors annually, drawn by its timeless beauty and symbol of enduring love.

KARNATAKA

Hampi, Karnataka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ancient ruins, temples, and stunning landscapes. Once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, it boasts iconic landmarks like the Virupaksha Temple and Vittala Temple. Visitors can explore its rich history, trek Matanga Hill for panoramic views, and enjoy coracle rides along the Tungabhadra River, making it a captivating destination for history buffs and nature lovers alike.

UTTARA KHAND

Rishikesh, nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas in Uttarakhand, India, is a spiritual and adventure hub. Known as the "Yoga Capital of the World," it attracts seekers from around the globe for its serene ambiance and numerous yoga and meditation centers. The sacred Ganges River flows through Rishikesh, offering opportunities for spiritual cleansing through rituals like Ganga Aarti. Additionally, it's a popular destination for adventure enthusiasts, with activities like white-water rafting, bungee jumping, and trekking in the surrounding hills. Rishikesh's unique blend of spirituality and adventure makes it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking both inner peace and adrenaline-pumping experiences.

RAJASTHAN

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan in India, is renowned for its vibrant culture, rich history, and stunning architecture. Known as the "Pink City" due to the distinct pink hue of its buildings, Jaipur is a treasure trove of palaces, forts, and markets. The majestic Amber Fort, Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), and City Palace are among its iconic landmarks. Jaipur's bustling bazaars offer an array of handicrafts, textiles, and gemstones, making it a shopper's paradise. The city's culinary scene is equally enticing, with mouthwatering Rajasthani cuisine served in its traditional eateries. With its blend of tradition and modernity, Jaipur continues to enchant visitors from around the world.
